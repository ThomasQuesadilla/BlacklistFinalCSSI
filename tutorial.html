Blacklist

Giving content control back to the user.

Our Mission

INFORMATION IS EVERYWHERE. More recorded information has been produced in the past two years than in all the previous history of the human race.

With a surplus of information so readily available, it is easy to come across content that one would not wish to view. Blacklist seeks to give users control over the content that they view on a daily basis through a user-friendly Chrome extension.

Tutorial

Step one – download the extension

Download the containing all the files that will make up the extension. Inside you’ll find three files:

  • content.js
  • manifest.json
  • README.md

Step two – enable Chrome developer mode & preview extension

Chrome rightly doesn’t allow the installation of extensions from anywhere other than the Chrome Web Store, but getting around that is as simple as visiting chrome://extensions and checking the box that says “Developer mode.” A couple new buttons will appear right below, and you want to click “Load unpacked extension,” which will open up to the file directory. Choose the older you downloaded and confirm it by clicking “Select.”

Now your extension should block your list of words!
