Blacklist

"Giving content control back to the user."

Our Mission

INFORMATION IS EVERYWHERE. More recorded information has been produced in the past two years than in all the previous history of the human race.

With a surplus of information so readily available on the internet, it is easy to come across content that one would not wish to view. Blacklist seeks to give users control over the content that they view on a daily basis through a user-friendly Chrome extension. The Chrome extension uses redaction to block out text that has been "blacklisted" so that the content is not visible to the users on any website that is viewed. Whether it is a veteran suffering PTSD attemping to avoid media coverage of war or a parent striving to monitor their child's online activity, Blacklist seeks to serve a diverse range of users.





Tutorial


Step One: Download the Extension

Click on the "Download extension" button below to download all the files that will make up the extension. A pop-up will appear at the bottom of your screen- click on this to view the downloaded folder titled "Blacklist Content" that will contain the files needed to run the extension.


Step Two: Enable Chrome Developer Mode

Chrome doesn’t allow the installation of extensions from anywhere other than the Chrome Web Store, but getting around that is as simple- copy "chrome://extensions" into your URL and check the box that says “Developer mode.”


Step Three: Preview Extension

A couple new buttons will appear right below, and you want to click “Load unpacked extension,” which will open up to the file directory. Choose the "Blacklist Content" folder you downloaded and confirm it by clicking “Select.”


Now your extension should block your list of words!


Step Four: Open the Options

Click on the options icon in the top right corner (it looks like a square with an o inside) of your browser. From here you can customize your options and enable the Blacklist of your choice.

  • Redaction Color: This will be the color that blocks out the content on the page. Choose from white, black, or customize your own color!
  • Click the checkbox to choose which Blacklist you want to enable.
Disable at any time by visiting chrome://extensions and unchecking the enable box.

Preset Blacklists

The following section gives more background info about the blacklists that are found within the extension as well as a sample of the blacklisted words.

General Trauma

When one faces domestic violence or suicide, it can be difficult to view certain content while coming to terms with what occured.This preset enables users to freely search the web as they recover from any trauma they faced without having to worry about viewing sensitive content.

Sample Words: violence, death, blood, assault

Resources:
  • Suicide Prevention Resource Center
  • The National Domestic Violence Hotline
  • The National Sexual Assault Telephone Hotline

Military PTSD

According to the U.S. Department of Veteran Affairs, veterans who experience PTSD may see their symptoms worsen if they are exposed to reminders of their experiences through media coverage.

This preset enables vets to browse the web freely without fear of coming across content that may raise stress levels and worsen PTSD symptoms.

Sample Words: war, terrorists, IED, gunshots, explosion

Resources:

  • U.S. Department of Veteran Affairs
  • Operation We Are Here

Alcohol Abuse

Individuals who have dealt with alcohol abuse have different triggers that may cause them to relapse. According to Recovery.org, one of these triggers is societal pressures.

This preset enables individuals to browse the web, including social media sites that may contain content that would make them more susceptible to relapse.


Sample Words: drinking, beer, social drinking

Resources:
  • Alcoholics Anonymous
  • SAMHSA

Parental Controls

According to the Pew Research Center, 1 in 3 parents are concerned about their children's digital habits. From learning "bad words" to being exposed to explicit content, parents must monitor the activities of their kids.

Parenting is hard. This preset makes your job a little easier by filtering out text content to protect your children while online.


Resources:
  • Safe Home
  • Unicef USA
